India Gate

India Gate, located in the heart of New Delhi, is a majestic war memorial built in honor of Indian soldiers who sacrificed their lives in World War I. This iconic archway is not only a symbol of national pride but also a popular spot for picnics and gatherings.

Red Fort

The Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, is an iconic Mughal fortress known for its stunning red sandstone architecture and historical significance as a symbol of India's rich heritage. Built in the 17th century, it was the main residence of Mughal emperors for over two centuries.

GuruDwara Bangla Sahib

Gurudwara Bangla Sahib, located in New Delhi, is a revered Sikh temple known for its serene atmosphere and its association with Guru Har Krishan, the eighth Sikh Guru. It features a shimmering sarovar (holy pond) and serves free meals to visitors of all backgrounds as a symbol of Sikh community service.

12:00 AM - 12:00 PM

Lotus Temple

The Lotus Temple, located in New Delhi, is a striking architectural marvel known for its lotus-shaped design and its role as a Bahá'í House of Worship, welcoming people of all faiths and backgrounds for prayer and meditation. This serene and inclusive place of worship is a symbol of unity and harmony

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, is a towering minaret that stands as a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture and a symbol of India's rich history. Built in the 12th century, it boasts intricate carvings and is a testament to the Delhi Sultanate's architectural legacy.

Akshardham Temple

Swaminarayan Akshardham is a Hindu temple, and spiritual-cultural campus in Delhi, India. The temple is close to the border with Noida.

Red Fort

The Red Fort or Lal Qila is a historic fort in the Old Delhi neighbourhood of Delhi, India, that historically served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors.

Qutub Minar

The Qutb Minar, also spelled Qutub Minar and Qutab Minar, is a minaret and "victory tower" that forms part of the Qutb complex, which lies at the site of Delhi’s oldest fortified city, Lal Kot, founded by the Tomar Rajputs.

Agrasen ki Baoli

Agrasen Ki Baoli is a 60-meter long and 15-meter wide historical stepwell in New Delhi, India.It was designated a protected monument by the Archaeological Survey of India

Jantar Mantar

Jantar Mantar is located in the modern city of New Delhi. "Jantar Mantar" means "instruments for measuring the harmony of the heavens". It consists of 13 architectural astronomy instruments.

Nehru Palace

Nehru Place houses the headquarters of several Indian firms and rivals with other financial centers in the metropolis like Connaught Place, Bhikaji Cama Place and Rajendra Place.

Connaught Place

Connaught Place is a frenetic business and financial hub, centered on a ring of colonnaded Georgian-style buildings with global chain stores, vintage cinemas, bars and Indian restaurants.

Garden of Five Senses

The Garden of Five Senses is a park in Delhi, India. Spread over 20 acres, the park is located in Saidul Ajaib village, opposite Saket, near the Mehrauli heritage area.
