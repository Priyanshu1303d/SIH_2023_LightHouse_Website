Mumbai

The Gateway of India, a prominent monument in Mumbai, stands as an iconic symbol of the city and India's colonial history. This historic arch overlooks the Arabian Sea and serves as a popular tourist attraction and a starting point for boat trips to Elephanta Island.

Kolhapur

The Mahalaxmi Temple in Kolhapur, Maharashtra, is a sacred Hindu shrine dedicated to Goddess Mahalaxmi, known for its architectural grandeur and historical significance, attracting devotees seeking blessings and spiritual solace. The temple features intricate stone carvings and stands as a symbol of devotion and cultural heritage

Aurangabad

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves are UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Maharashtra, India, renowned for their ancient rock-cut cave temples and monasteries, featuring exquisite sculptures and frescoes that depict Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain religious themes. These caves are a testament to India's rich artistic and religious history, dating back to as early as the 2nd century BCE.

Lonavala

Lonavala, a picturesque hill station in Maharashtra, India, is renowned for its lush green landscapes, cascading waterfalls, and serene atmosphere, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and a perfect retreat from city life.

Pune

The Aga Khan Palace, located in Pune, India, is a historic landmark known for its architectural elegance and its role in India's struggle for independence. Built in 1892, it houses a museum dedicated to Mahatma Gandhi and serves as a significant monument in the country's history.
